Detailed Description

Computes the critical buckling load of a column by means of J.B. Johnson's equation.

Remarks
The J.B. Johnson buckling equation is as follows: \( P_{cr} = A (S_{y} - (\frac{S_{y} R_{s}}{2 \pi})^2 (\frac{1}{E})) \); where \( R_{s} = \frac{L_{e}}{\rho} \), and \( \rho = \sqrt{\frac{I}{A}} \).
  • \( P_{cr} = \) The critical buckling load.
  • \( S_{y} = \) The yield strength of the material.
  • \( E = \) The modulus of elasticity of the material.
  • \( L_{e} = \) The effective length of the column.
  • \( I = \) The moment of inertia (area) of the column (softest axis).
  • \( A = \) The cross-sectional area of the column.
